In the high-energy reality of the 31st century, the story of humankind is written in physics. Our ancestors lived under a thin, forgiving sky; we live under the Heavy Earth, an atmosphere so thick with energy and turbulence that it is a physical force, a constant resistance to motion and life. The following vignettes are not tales of grand battles or heroic discovery. They are glimpses into the "boring" reality of this life—the quiet, intimate, and often stressful moments of maintenance, negotiation, and interdependence that define our existence.
